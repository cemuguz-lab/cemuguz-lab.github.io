About Me

Evolution of My Research

Bridging Physics and AI for Molecular Discovery

With a PhD in Physics, specializing in Computational Physics, my doctoral research focused on understanding the behavior of molecular systems through molecular dynamics simulations. This foundational work in physics provided me with a robust analytical and computational skill set, which I later augmented with extensive learning and application of Artificial Intelligence (AI) technologies. This journey equipped me with the ability to apply AI to complex scientific problems, particularly in molecular science.

Since August 2024, I've been a full-time researcher at the Center for Computational Drug Design (HiTMer). Here, I develop novel AI projects from the ground up. My work spans from building AI-powered molecular modeling frameworks to addressing the generalization challenges of purely data-driven AI models in physical systems. This has led me to explore and develop Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs). I strongly believe that integrating PINNs with diffusion models will enable more realistic and effective outcomes in computational drug design projects using generative AI. I am dedicated to expanding my expertise and career in this exciting, interdisciplinary field.

Beyond my primary research responsibilities, I have independently initiated and led several high-impact projects, including a 3D conformer generator, a GNN-based affinity prediction framework (CNNAffinity), and a novel sonification approach for molecular systems. These projects reflect my commitment to innovation, reproducibility, and scientific communication. I maintain a strong focus on integrating physics principles into AI models, and actively contribute to open-source and academic communities through tutorials, code, and collaborative research. My long-term goal is to establish robust, physics-aware AI systems that can accelerate real-world molecular discovery.
